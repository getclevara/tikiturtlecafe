What We Believe

Work is dignity. A kitchen can be a classroom.

We believe people with disabilities don't need to be protected from real jobs. They need real jobs built for them. Tiki Turtle Cafe is a program of The Arc of Hilo, and for three years it's been proof that this works.

Now we want to build something bigger: an ecosystem where training leads to certification, certification leads to a career, and our kitchen becomes a resource the whole food community in Hilo can use.

That starts with the people already cooking in our kitchen today.

Meet The People This Kitchen Is For

A bigger kitchen isn't the goal. It's for people like these.

A certified teaching kitchen isn't an upgrade for its own sake. Here's who it's actually for, in their own program, cooking real food, every day.

J

Jace

Team Lead, Front of House

Jace started in Hawaiʻi Community College's culinary program. The pace, and what the program expected of him, didn't match how he learns best. So he came home to Tiki Turtle Cafe. He started in the back of the house, learning the line one order at a time. Today he runs the front of house, and people stop by just to talk with him.

He didn't need a different kind of talent. He needed a kitchen built around how he learns.
E

Ethan

Team Member

Ethan graduated high school with a hunger for more. He didn't want to fall into a system with no next step. He wanted to learn. He joined Tiki Turtle Cafe and found his spark: cooking. Ethan lives with seizures, and he doesn't let them set his limits. He shows up, he cooks, and he thrives.

L

Lisa

Team Member

Lisa has enough joy for a hundred people. She tried a few other jobs before Tiki Turtle Cafe, and none of them fit. Here, she found her rhythm, especially this past year. She's stepped up to prep, to cook, to build a real sandwich from scratch.

Her autism isn't something she overcomes. It's part of how she thrives.
G

Genesis

Team Member

Genesis was born with a cleft palate and autism. People sometimes read her as shy. What they miss is that she's a fast learner and an even faster problem solver. Whatever we ask of her, she tries. She's learned to make hot dogs, baked goods, musubi, and she goes home and cooks full meals for her family. She's already talking about starting her own food business.

D

Dayton

Team Member

Dayton moved out on his own and went looking for a job that would let him build real independence. He found it in our kitchen. Ask anyone on our team: his attention to detail, and the quality he puts into every plate, stand out.

Z

Zevin

Team Member

Zevin is quiet. He's not always verbal, and it's easy to overlook him if you don't know to look. Put him in his element, somewhere he feels safe, and you watch him excel. He came to us through a school work program, one of several places that had already written him off. Our chef and our leadership watched him come alive in the kitchen and knew: we need him on our team.

A

Chef Angelina

Head Chef & Mentor

Chef Angelina has come through her own share of hardship. Cooking became her way through it, and now it's her way of serving others. She leads our kitchen with the patience of someone who knows exactly what it's like to need it.

A

Allyssa

Team Lead & Mentor

Allyssa trains our front of house team and is the steady hand many of our participants lean on. She's the one who makes sure every new team member has someone in their corner.

These are eight of the reasons we're building a bigger kitchen. There will be more names next year, if we can build the room for them.

A paycheck was never the point. It's a mom watching her son run a kitchen. A family getting their person back, thriving. Lives changed. Families changed. For good.

Where We've Been

We already proved we could do this.

Three years ago, Tiki Turtle Cafe opened as a small food stand. Since then we've built our way to a working prep kitchen, made more than 23,500 meals, and earned over $425,000 doing it, all while training a team most employers had overlooked.

We built this ourselves, one shift at a time. Now we're ready for the next course.

The Next Course

From prep kitchen to teaching kitchen.

A prep kitchen can feed people. A certified kitchen can teach people, and it can serve our whole community. Here's what we're building next.

01

A certified teaching kitchen

We'll bring our current prep kitchen up to full commercial certification, with three dedicated teaching stations so our team can train side by side instead of one at a time.

02

A real path to certification

We're building a partnership with Hawaiʻi Community College's culinary program, so our participants can earn credentials that count, not just experience that stays inside our walls.

03

A kitchen for the community

Local chefs and food vendors in Hilo need certified kitchen space and can't always find it. Once we're certified, we can open our kitchen to them too, and turn Tiki Turtle into a resource the whole food community shares.
